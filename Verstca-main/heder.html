Niigata is a beautiful city on the shores of the Sea of ​​Japan. Rice cultivation is the basis of the local agriculture. The Niigata area has numerous rice fields. Moreover, this port city is a bustling transportation hub, easily accessible by train, plane, or boat.

OSAKA

221
50

Osaka isn't very far from Tokyo, but the city has a completely different character. It boasts a vibrant nightlife, delicious food, and open-hearted and welcoming residents. Historic buildings, the centerpiece of which is the castle, stand alongside countless shops and modern attractions.

SEE MORE

SAITAMA

221
50

During the Edo period, Saitama was a major transportation hub and a stopover on the route to Nikko, but today it's effectively a suburb of Tokyo. Many of its historic towns, such as Kawagoe, retain a traditional feel. Saitama is close enough to Tokyo that its main attractions are easily accessible from the capital.

SEE MORE

UENO

221
50

Ueno is a small country with its own unique flora and fauna, history and culture, traditions and festivals. Ueno is one of the most famous and visited parks in the Japanese capital and is also one of the first five public parks established in the country after the Meiji Restoration (1868).

SEE MORE

SHIBUYA

221
50

Shopping in Shibuya is exceptional. With world-class department stores located near Shinjuku, Shibuya is the unofficial capital of eclectic shopping. It sells the most bizarre goods: niche clothing, various music records, incredible Japanese inventions. Whatever you're looking for, you'll find it in Shibuya.
